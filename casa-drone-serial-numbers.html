How to find drone, model aircraft and RPA serial numbers

Finding the right serial number is important to ensure your drone details are registered properly. The serial number is a unique identifier that will help CASA, police or other authorities know your drone is yours and legally registered.

Where can I find my serial number?

My drone is a DJI

If you have a DJI drone, read specific guidance on how to find the correct serial number in our DJI serial number guide.

My drone is another manufacturer

Most drones will have their serial number written on or inside the body of the drone. Follow these steps to locate the right serial number:

  • Ensure your drone is powered down and in a safe state for inspection
  • Find the location of the battery on the body of the drone
  • Remove the battery from the drone and inspect the battery compartment. Look for a range of numbers or characters. This number may also be labelled simply as 'Serial number' or 'S/N'.
  • If your drone does not have a separate battery, inspect the body of the drone for a range of numbers or characters as above
  • You may also find the serial number on your warranty card or original packaging

What if I can't find a serial number, or my drone, RPA or model aircraft doesn't have one?

During registration you can check a box labelled 'No serial number available'. CASA will then give you a CASA-generated mark which you must affix to your drone. Find out more on how to display and affix this mark.
